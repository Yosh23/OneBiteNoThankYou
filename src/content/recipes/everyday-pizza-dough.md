---
title: Everyday Pizza Dough
description: One dough, a hundred uses. Makes enough for two 12-inch pizzas or a sheet pan.

servings: 2
prepTime: 15
cookTime: 0
ingredients:
  - amount: 3.5
    unit: cup
    name: bread flour (or all-purpose), plus more for dusting
  - amount: 1.5
    unit: tsp
    name: instant yeast
  - amount: 1.5
    unit: tsp
    name: fine salt
  - amount: 1
    unit: tsp
    name: granulated sugar
  - amount: 1.25
    unit: cup
    name: lukewarm water
  - amount: 2
    unit: tbsp
    name: olive oil, plus more for the bowl
notes: For the best flavor, mix the dough the night before and let it rise slowly in the fridge for 12–24 hours. Bring to room temp for an hour before shaping.
addedBy: Me
---

1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, yeast, salt, and sugar.

2. Add the water and olive oil. Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms.

3. Turn out onto a lightly floured counter and knead for **8–10 minutes**, until smooth and elastic. The dough should feel tacky but not stick to your hand.

4. Lightly oil a large bowl, place the dough in, and turn to coat. Cover with a damp towel or plastic wrap.

5. Let rise in a warm spot for about **1.5 hours**, until doubled in size. (Or refrigerate overnight — see notes.)

6. Punch down gently and divide in half. Each half makes one 12-inch pizza.

7. Use immediately, or refrigerate the dough balls for up to 3 days, or freeze for up to 3 months.
